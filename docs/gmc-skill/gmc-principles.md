# GMC Principles

## Mindset

Use a defensive compliance posture. A store should look like a stable, real business with consistent policies and product data, not like a persuasive landing page.

Misrepresentation reviews are usually not about one magic phrase. They are about trust consistency:

- Who is selling?
- Where can the customer contact the seller?
- What is being sold?
- What will the customer pay?
- Where is delivery available?
- How long does delivery take?
- What happens with returns, refunds, damaged goods, cancellation, and duties/taxes?
- Does the product feed match the website?
- Does the website make claims it cannot prove?

## Zero-Invention Rule

Never add a claim just because it sounds reassuring. A claim is allowed only if it is:

1. true,
2. visible or enforceable in a policy/setting,
3. consistent across site and GMC,
4. not likely to be contradicted at checkout or by product data.

If a claim is useful but not yet supported, first add the exact support in policies/settings, then echo the same wording elsewhere.

## High-Risk Claim Classes

Remove or rewrite these unless they are objectively verified and necessary:

- review counts, stars, Trustpilot references, testimonials, ratings
- "trusted by", "certified", "approved", "official", "best", "number 1"
- warranty/guarantee claims not matched by a policy
- "free", "no fees", "taxes included", "duties included" unless exactly true for the target country and checkout
- "delivery in X days" if policy, GMC shipping, and checkout do not match
- scarcity or urgency: "limited stock", "only X left", countdowns
- unrealistic discounts or compare-at prices
- medical, safety, performance, or income claims
- "secure payment" badges that are decorative or unsupported
- statements that a thing appears in cart/checkout if it does not

## Safer Wording Patterns

Use exact, neutral language:

- `The UK shipping cost is £0.00.`
- `Orders are delivered to the United Kingdom in 5 - 7 business days.`
- `Returns are accepted within 14 days according to the return policy.`
- `Return shipping costs are paid by the customer unless otherwise required by law.`
- `Contact support at contact@example.com.`
- `Product images, selected variant, delivery method, and return information should be checked before ordering.`

Avoid vague marketing claims:

- `Fast delivery`
- `Free delivery worldwide`
- `Guaranteed satisfaction`
- `Best quality`
- `Trusted by thousands`

## Required Trust Surfaces

A GMC-safe store should have accessible, coherent pages for:

- Contact
- Shipping or delivery policy
- Return/refund policy
- Terms of sale or terms of service
- Privacy policy
- Legal notice or business identity page where relevant
- Track order only if the tracking feature works

Footer/navigation should place policies under policy/legal sections, not hide them.

## Business Identity

The business identity must be stable across site, policies, Shopify, and GMC:

- business name
- legal operator name, if different from brand
- business address
- customer service email
- customer service phone, if displayed
- customer service hours, if displayed
- domain

If the business is registered in one country and sells in another, state that plainly. Do not pretend local incorporation.

## Market, Currency, Taxes, and Duties

For every target market:

- site currency must match product feed currency for that market
- GMC shipping service currency must match product currency
- policies must explain target countries clearly
- duties/taxes/import handling must be stated only if true
- price-related wording must not contradict checkout

For a single-market UK store:

- Shopify store currency, feed label, product feed, site, and GMC should all use GBP.
- GMC country should be United Kingdom only unless more markets are truly configured.

## Shipping and Returns

Shipping policy should include:

- target country/countries
- processing time
- order cutoff if available
- delivery time
- shipping cost
- carrier/tracking only if true
- duties/taxes/import handling if relevant

Refund/return policy should include:

- return window
- eligibility conditions
- damaged goods process
- refund processing time
- return shipping cost responsibility
- contact method

GMC shipping and return settings must mirror these values.

## Product Feed Consistency

Product feed must match live product pages:

- title
- price
- currency
- availability
- URL
- image
- variant
- description
- brand/vendor
- country and language
- shipping and return handling

After any Shopify SEO/product change, verify that the same value appears inside GMC product detail. If not, force feed app sync or wait.

## Review Readiness

The site is ready for review only when an auditor can click through and find no contradiction. A clean Shopify site is not enough if GMC still shows stale product data.
